Part One: The Story

Plusser is a fun tool for women to connect with other women “just like them” who have similar body types and/or interests and could recommend clothes, items and experiences they loved.


The experience of connecting to objects and shopping is so awkward online, based on a cookie-cutter model that does not reflect either how people look, what they may actually need or like, or what they actually bought, love and vouch for.

The only people you can really trust, who know what works for you, are people just like you, with similar lifestyles, pain points and preferences, not "celebrities", "influencers" or "brands".

People like just like you.

Speak up for what they love.

Part Two: Understanding users'needs

Designing with and for the user


From Pain Point to Product is a 5-step writing process used to transform one specific user pain point into a product solution.

Before getting into design and architecture, it's crucial to really understand user's needs. This is a process I created to really go in-depth into understanding users' needs and working from there in the design process.

The 5-step pain point to product process can be used to write out solutions, tasks, user stories and possible product features as they relate to soothing a given pain point.

From Pain Point to Product Worksheet

USER NEEDS TOP ↑

Pain Point Card

1. PAIN POINT EXERCISE

A) Outline the solution to the user pain points by describing the exact opposite (positive) outcome, in everyday speech, i.e, in the user’s words.

B) Translate this statement into the third person, as an objective description of the user’s problem.

Pain Relief Card

2. PAIN RELIEF/SOLUTION EXERCISE

A) Outline the solution to the user pain points by describing the exact opposite (positive) outcome, in the user’s words.

B) Translate this statement into the third person, as an objective description of the user’s problem.

User Tasks & Habits

3. FROM USER TASK TO HABIT EXERCISE

A) Given the pain point and the pain relief outlined in the previous answer, describe the task the user will accomplish through the product, in order to get rid of her pain point.

B) Assuming the user accomplishes the task outlined above through the product with ease, what would make the user form a habit? Why would the user come back (in her own words)? What consistent attributes would the user highlight when describing the product to her friends?

User Stories

4. USER’S STORIES - EXERCISE

A) At this stage we have made the assumptions that the user utilizes the product regularly and that she has formed a *habit*. After the habit, comes the expectation of a standard of experience.

Extrapolating from the Agile Method’s “As a user I want to ___ so that ___” template as a way to further understand the user’s perspective and potential relationship with the product, we’ll use instead: “As a regular user of product X, I do X ____ through the product so that I can ___.”

Product Architecture

5. PRODUCT ARCHITECTURE - EXERCISE

This is meant as a very quick brainstorm, just so we can have a wide scope of possibilities for the initial design. The answers should not be detailed: just write down the first thing that comes to your mind. A verb, or an action would suffice.

What features would enable the user to accomplish her task? Where would these features be located within the overall architecture?
